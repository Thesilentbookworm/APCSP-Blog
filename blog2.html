The computing innovation I chose is Twitter which revolutionized how people are exposed to information about current political, economical, and other situations. Twitter is designed to provide a space for many to be kept in the sphere to update current events and trends through tweets. The function of Twitter happens when a client types in the text box where they would then request to send and post their tweet on the website under their profile for many to see. 2c:
A beneficial effect Twitter has is the ability to provide a service where political leaders and organizations are able to keep others in the know. According to the company's CMO, Leslie Berland, Twitter mainly focuses on providing the ability for their users to tweet blurbs about information that woukd be exposed to others keeping them in the loop, which has a more effect on the younger generation than regular news stations do. For example, if a political organization or movement, such as the #MeToo movement, wanted more exposure and notice of the public, then they would go on Twitter and provide a tweet while referencing their organization with a '#___', thus allowing many to notice these political events, movements, organizations, etc. and being able to find more current information through Twitter.

One harmful effect Twitter has is how it is used. Many users of Twitter post abhorrent tweets that are aimed at others, and is often used to bad-mouth different people. For example, our current U.S President, Donald J. Trump, uses Twitter to publish discriminating tweets targeted toward other ethnicites and races in order to incite his fanbase and supporters. According to an article on CNN, it was said that Trump had tweeted how he refers to Elizabeth Warren, a Native American presedential candidate, as Pocahontas and he'd see her on the 'TRAIL' referring to the Trail of Tears. As such, Twitter can be used to negatively influence others in doing the same thing, whilst attacking others.


2d:
The kind of data Twitter uses is the important information needed for the tweet to be 'tweeted' on a user's or Twitter's home page. Twitter would read the user's information required to know who tweeted what, when they tweeted it, and if they met the maximum set for characters. These types of information tell Twitter's servers exactly what to display on the home page of the user. For example, if someone tweeted 'This is my first TWEET!!', then the server would be able to send the information back with the data of: the user's display name and user ID, their photo, and time stamp. This would tell the other users who viewed this tweet who posted the tweet, when they posted it, and what their profile picture is. In other words, the input would be the information that is necessary for an individual to post a tweet, leading for the end result of the tweet, time stamp, display name, and any tagged information to be displayed.

One concern on Twitter is data security that can eventually lead to financial risks. Many users provide their personal information, such as their current address, passwords, etc., on their profiles which can be accessed by anyone who follows them even if the user's profile is private. If someone were to gain access to their private information, then they would be able to expose this security breach and use this chance to further gain access to the user's other accounts, such as their banking accounts.
